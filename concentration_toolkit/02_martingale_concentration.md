# Martingale Concentration

## Why RL Cannot Use I.I.D. Bounds

Data in RL is generated by the algorithm that is learning from it: the
policy that chooses `A_t` depends on everything seen before `t`. Formally,
with `F_t = sigma(S_0, A_0, R_1, ..., S_t)`, the summands of interest
(TD errors, regret increments, estimation residuals) are **adapted** and at
best **conditionally centered**:

```math
\mathbb{E}\big[X_t\mid\mathcal{F}_{t-1}\big]=0
\qquad\text{(martingale difference sequence, MDS)},
```

not independent. Every "with high probability" statement in
`finite_time_td_q/` and `regret_and_exploration/` runs through the two
inequalities below, which replace independence by conditional centering.

## Azuma–Hoeffding

**Theorem.** If `(X_t)` is an MDS with `|X_t| <= c_t` a.s., then for
`S_n = sum_{t<=n} X_t`:

```math
\Pr\{S_n\ge t\}\ \le\ \exp\Big(\frac{-t^2}{2\sum_{t=1}^n c_t^2}\Big).
```

*Proof.* The Chernoff method survives because conditioning peels terms one
at a time:

```math
\mathbb{E}\big[e^{\lambda S_n}\big]
=
\mathbb{E}\Big[e^{\lambda S_{n-1}}\ \underbrace{\mathbb{E}\big[e^{\lambda X_n}\mid\mathcal{F}_{n-1}\big]}_{\le\ \exp(\lambda^2c_n^2/2)}\Big]
\ \le\
\mathbb{E}\big[e^{\lambda S_{n-1}}\big]\,e^{\lambda^2c_n^2/2},
```

the inner bound by Hoeffding's lemma applied to the *conditional* law
(which is centered by the MDS property and supported in `[-c_n, c_n]`).
Induct, then optimize `lambda` as in notebook 01. ∎

**Load-bearing audit:** independence was used in notebook 01 only to
factorize the mgf; the tower property + conditional Hoeffding replaces it
exactly. What is *lost*: the constants can no longer exploit per-term
asymmetric ranges as sharply, and — more importantly — the bound still pays
the worst-case range `c_t`, motivating Freedman.

## Freedman's Inequality

The martingale Bernstein: pay the *realized* conditional variance.

**Theorem.** `(X_t)` an MDS with `X_t <= M` a.s.; define the predictable
quadratic variation

```math
V_n=\sum_{t=1}^n\mathbb{E}\big[X_t^2\mid\mathcal{F}_{t-1}\big].
```

Then for all `t >= 0, v > 0`:

```math
\Pr\big\{S_n\ge t\ \text{and}\ V_n\le v\big\}
\ \le\
\exp\Big(\frac{-t^2}{2(v+Mt/3)}\Big).
```

*Proof core.* Define, for fixed `lambda in (0, 3/M)`,

```math
Z_n=\exp\Big(\lambda S_n-\frac{\lambda^2}{2(1-\lambda M/3)}V_n\Big).
```

Claim: `(Z_n)` is a **supermartingale**. Indeed,

```math
\mathbb{E}[Z_n\mid\mathcal{F}_{n-1}]
=
Z_{n-1}\,\exp\Big(-\frac{\lambda^2\,\mathbb{E}[X_n^2\mid\mathcal{F}_{n-1}]}{2(1-\lambda M/3)}\Big)\ \mathbb{E}\big[e^{\lambda X_n}\mid\mathcal{F}_{n-1}\big]
\ \le\ Z_{n-1},
```

by the Bernstein mgf bound of notebook 01 applied conditionally — the
per-step mgf inflation is *exactly cancelled* by the subtracted variance
process. Then `E[Z_n] <= Z_0 = 1`, and Markov on the event
`{S_n >= t, V_n <= v}` (where `Z_n >= exp(lambda t - lambda^2 v / (2(1 -
lambda M/3)))`) plus the optimal `lambda` gives the bound. ∎

The supermartingale construction — *exponential of the sum, discounted by
its own variance process* — is the single most reused proof device in
modern RL theory: it is the engine inside the self-normalized bound
(notebook 03), inside Bernstein-bonus regret proofs
(`regret_and_exploration/03`), and inside law-of-iterated-logarithm-style
anytime confidence sequences.

## Two Practical Corollaries RL Consumes

**1. Anytime version via peeling.** Freedman holds for a fixed `n`; regret
proofs need all `n` simultaneously. Union-bound over geometric "epochs"
`V_n in [2^k, 2^{k+1})` (peeling) to get: with probability `1 - delta`, for
all `n` simultaneously,

```math
S_n\ \le\ 2\sqrt{V_n\,\log\frac{\log_2 (V_n \vee 2)}{\delta}}+\ \tfrac{2M}{3}\log\tfrac{\cdot}{\delta}\quad\text{(up to constants)},
```

the `log log` being the price of anytime validity.

**2. Bounded-increment episodic sums.** For episodic RL with `H` steps and
increments in `[0, H]` per episode, Azuma gives deviations of order
`H sqrt(K log(1/delta))` after `K` episodes; Freedman upgrades `H` to the
per-episode conditional standard deviation — the seed of the
`sqrt(H^3) -> sqrt(H^2 * H)` bookkeeping in `regret_and_exploration/03`.

## The Optional Stopping Caveat

RL analyses constantly evaluate sums at **random times** (the `k`-th visit
to a state, the end of a random-length episode). The supermartingale
argument survives arbitrary stopping times (`E[Z_tau] <= 1` by optional
stopping for nonnegative supermartingales), which is precisely why the
"per-visit-count" confidence intervals of `regret_and_exploration/02` are
legitimate. The illegitimate move — building the interval from a count
chosen *because* the estimate looked extreme, without a uniform bound — is
the classic bug the peeling/union step exists to prevent.

## What Remains Open

Closed classical material; the frontier is empirical-Bernstein variants
(variance estimated from the same data — used in
`regret_and_exploration/03`) whose constants are still being sharpened.
